# Research Plan — Venmo Group Pay Later

*Scaffolded artifact. Full plan available on request. The four sections below are load-bearing; the rest is structure.*

## Objectives

- Validate group-splitting behavior at frequency and basket-size levels that make Group Pay Later economically meaningful.
- Measure privacy-tolerance on financial-content feed events across Venmo 18–35 segments.
- De-risk the Ask-a-Friend recovery UX against CFPB-adjacent perception risks flagged in the [council transcript](../validation/council-transcript.md) and [business panel](../validation/business-panel.md).

## Audience Segmentation (8 segments)

| Segment | Why we care |
|---|---|
| 18–24 early-career, event-heavy | Coachella archetype — highest Group Pay Later affinity hypothesis |
| 18–24 college cohort | Generational-assist archetype, splits rent / groceries / group travel |
| 25–29 urban professional | Highest BNPL market share in Venmo core |
| 25–29 couples / roommates | Travel + furniture archetype |
| 30–35 early-parent | Group-gifting archetype; more privacy-conscious on feed |
| 30–35 non-parent | Same as 25–29, higher basket size |
| Power Venmo P2P user (10+ txns/mo) | Viral-loop engine; where K ≥ 0.4 has to come from |
| Low-frequency Venmo user (<2 txns/mo) | Conversion challenge; proves the surface works beyond power users |

## Methods (brief)

- **Moderated interviews:** 6 per segment × 8 = 48 sessions, Day 15–45 of 30-60-90.
- **12-question survey:** fielded to a balanced Venmo MAU panel (n ≥ 1,200).
- **Diary study:** 20 participants for 3 weeks, logging group-purchase events (real or hypothetical) with and without Group Pay Later.
- **Concept test:** unmoderated usability on the clickable prototype (6 flows, 60 sessions).
- **Existing research audit:** Week 1 inventory of prior Venmo UXR studies on feed visibility, P2P friction, BNPL awareness.

## Sample Interview Guide (excerpt)

1. Walk me through the last time you split a purchase with friends. What worked? What was frustrating?
2. If one friend couldn't pay you back for 2 weeks, what did you do?
3. Have you ever used BNPL (Affirm, Klarna, Afterpay, Pay in 4)? When and why?
4. If a purchase you made with friends showed up in a social feed, what would make that feel OK vs not OK?
5. If a friend asked you to cover their installment on a BNPL plan, how would you feel?

*(Full guide: 24 questions, branching logic, 45 min per session. Available on request.)*

## Success Criteria

- ≥60% of interviewed users confirm the pain point in the Concert / Travel / Gift archetypes.
- ≥25% comfortable with "friends-only" feed visibility on a group purchase; <15% acceptance on "public" would invalidate the feed thesis.
- Ask-a-Friend UX rated "helpful, not coercive" by ≥70% of concept-test respondents.
