- **3 targeted interviews** with heavy group-purchase users (festivals, travel, group gifting).
- **Existing research audit** — any prior Venmo UXR studies on feed visibility, P2P request friction, BNPL awareness.

### Deliverables by Day 30

### What I will NOT do in Days 1–30

## Days 31–60: Narrow & Decide

**Theme:** Stop listening, start deciding. One load-bearing decision: what ships first.

### The decision

Based on Day 1–30 findings — and grounded in the [council's pragmatist critique](../validation/council-transcript.md) that Phase 1 in Q1 is not a survivable commitment — the default recommendation is:

> **Phase 1 ships first as solo Pay in 4 on Venmo, underwritten by PayPal Lending, Inc., inheriting [PPL PRG §17](../../PayPal-PayLater/APM-PRG-PayPal-PayLater.md) state license footprint. Group Pay Later is a Year-2 differentiation thesis that Phase 1 earns the right to build.**

This is a revision to the pitch's original framing. The revision is deliberate — the pitch's Q1/Q2 timeline does not survive PayPal's real shipping cadence, and the load-bearing assumption is state-license inheritance, not product novelty.

## Risks I Will Surface Early (before they surface me)

1. **Merchant acceptance is the single biggest launch risk.** Venmo has no merchant-fee muscle memory. Onboarding 5 merchants at 4.9% in 90 days is optimistic without proactive Commerce Platform partnership. I raise this Day 1 with the Director, not Day 90 when Q1 KRs are at risk.
2. **CFPB regulatory shift between pitch and launch.** The May 2024 Interpretive Rule is the current surface; any tightening on group-invite UDAAP or periodic-statement obligations could delay Phase 2 by 6–12 months. I set up a monthly Legal-briefing to monitor, not a quarterly one.
3. **PayPal Pay Later team may view Venmo BNPL as cannibalization.** The stakeholder alignment on this needs to happen in Week 1 with the PPL Product Lead, not Week 12. The framing: Venmo extends PPL's reach into the 18–35 cohort where PPL underperforms; it does not compete.
4. **Correlated-default risk in group cohorts.** Flagged by both the [business panel](../validation/business-panel.md) (Taleb lens) and the [council's critic](../validation/council-transcript.md). I bring this to the Risk & Credit team in Day 30, not after the first default cluster.
5. **State-license inheritance may not be automatic.** If PayPal Lending, Inc.'s state MTLs do not cleanly extend to the Venmo surface (some states treat the branded consumer-facing surface as relevant to the MTL scope), Phase 1 timing slips. I get this answer in Day 7 with the Underwriting Lead.

## How I'll Know I'm Failing (and What I Do About It)

- **Day 30** — I don't have a read on Commerce Platform's merchant pilot capacity → escalate to Director, not panic.
- **Day 45** — PayPal Lending, Inc. can't confirm clean state-license inheritance → revise Y1 OKRs, do not pretend.
- **Day 60** — 3 pilot LOIs are not signed → push Phase 1 launch to Q3, communicate clearly.
- **Day 85** — Early delinquency is above PPL baseline + 100 bps → pause cohort expansion, do not rationalize.

The failure mode I avoid at all costs: shipping on schedule while hiding a worsening credit signal. Credit products don't fail slowly; they fail suddenly after looking fine for 8 weeks.

## Guiding Principle for the First 90 Days

Per the [council transcript](../validation/council-transcript.md): the pitch's original Q1/Q2 timeline is not survivable in a pressure interview. In the seat, I replace the pitch's cadence with a 2-year thesis — **solo Pay in 4 on Venmo shipped credibly in H1 is the Q1 deliverable; Group Pay Later validated at scale in H2 is the differentiation thesis; Year 2 is where the moat is earned.** This replaces theatrical speed with shipping discipline, which is what PayPal's "profitable growth" posture rewards.
